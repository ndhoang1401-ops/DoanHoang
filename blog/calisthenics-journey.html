3 Years of Calisthenics: From Zero to Advanced

Feb 1, 2024 • 10 min read • Personal Growth

On the same day I wrote my first line of code in 2023, I couldn't do 5 pull-ups. Three years later, I can hold an L-sit for 30 seconds, execute muscle-ups, and train 6 days a week. This is the story of my calisthenics journey — and the unexpected parallels with learning to code.

Year 0: The Beginning

January 2023. I was weak. Not in spirit, but in body. I could barely pull myself up 5 times on a bar. My first training session was humbling:

  • Pull-ups: 3 reps (strict form, no momentum)
  • Push-ups: 15 reps (on knees after day 5)
  • Dips: 0 reps (couldn't even lower myself slowly)

But I was determined. I started training 3 days a week, following a beginner program focused on fundamentals: proper form, consistent volume, and progressive overload.

Year 1: Building Foundations

Progress was steady but slow. By month 3, I could finally do 10 clean pull-ups. By month 6, I hit 15. The key was consistency — never skipping workouts, always prioritizing form over reps.

In this year, I learned:

  • Consistency beats intensity. 3 good sessions per week beat 1 epic session per month.
  • Form is non-negotiable. Clean reps build better foundations than ego-lifting.
  • Recovery matters. Sleep, nutrition, and rest days are part of the training.

Year 2: Skill Development

With a solid foundation, I started learning advanced movements:

  • Handstand: 2 minutes freestanding (took 3 months of daily practice)
  • Muscle-up: First rep in month 8 of year 2 (euphoria!)
  • L-sit: 15 seconds hold (progressing to 30+ seconds)
  • Planche: Advanced tuck (still working on full planche)

This year taught me patience. Some movements took months of practice with zero visible results. Then suddenly — a breakthrough. Just like debugging code.

Year 3: Mastery & Consistency

Present day (2026). I train 5-6 days a week, splitting volume across:

  • Pull day: chin-ups, muscle-ups, weight pulls
  • Push day: dips, handstand training, planche progressions
  • Legs day: pistol squats, explosive jumps
  • Skill day: advanced movements, conditioning

Numbers aren't everything, but here's where I stand:

  • Pull-ups: 25+ strict reps
  • Muscle-ups: 8 reps in a set
  • Dips: 35+ reps weighted (10kg)
  • Handstand hold: 3+ minutes
  • L-sit: 30 seconds+ hold

The Coding Parallel

Here's what struck me: learning calisthenics and learning to code follow the exact same path:

1. Fundamentals First

In calisthenics, you master pull-ups before muscle-ups. In coding, you learn loops before frameworks. Both require boring, repetitive practice.

2. Progressive Overload

In the gym, you add 1 more rep each week. In coding, you build slightly harder projects each month. Small, consistent progress compounds.

3. Plateau Breaks Through Practice

I've been stuck on movements for weeks, seeing zero improvement. Then one day—breakthrough. Code is the same. You debug for hours, then suddenly understand the bug.

4. Form & Quality Matter

Bad form in pull-ups leads to injury. Bad practices in coding lead to technical debt. Both require discipline to maintain standards.

5. Community Accelerates Growth

Training with others pushes me harder. Learning with other developers (open source, communities) accelerates growth exponentially.

Mental Transformation

Beyond physical changes, calisthenics transformed my mindset:

  • Discomfort became a teacher, not something to avoid
  • Progress became about consistency, not perfection
  • Patience became a superpower
  • Discipline in the gym spilled into discipline at the desk

What I Would Tell Day-1 Me

"You won't see results for 3 months. At month 6, you'll be amazed. At year 2, people will ask for your secret. At year 3, you'll realize the secret is just time and consistency."

Also: "The muscle-up IS possible. And it's worth every rep you take to get there."

The Journey Continues

I'm still not satisfied. Next goals:

  • Full planche hold (10 seconds)
  • One-arm pull-up progressions
  • Back lever hold (20 seconds)
  • Maintain strength while reducing body fat

But unlike early 2023, I'm not anxious about these goals. I know the path. It requires time, consistency, and listening to my body. I'll get there.

Same as I did with code. Same as anything worth doing.
